MEET LAUREN

Real Estate at the Highest Level Is Not About Houses.

It is about reading a room before anyone speaks. It is about understanding what a family is actually trying to build, and which of two beautiful properties will quietly hold that life better. After eighteen years and more than two hundred transactions, that is the work I have learned to do well.

I spent the first decade of my career studying Park Cities one block at a time. I know which streets keep their light through the afternoon, which builders age with grace, and which homes are owned by people who will never list publicly but might, given the right call. I think about real estate the way a good interior designer thinks about a room, in proportion, restraint, and the quiet logic of how a life actually unfolds inside walls.

My practice is intentionally small. I take a limited number of clients each year so that every introduction, negotiation, and closing receives my direct attention. That is the only way I know how to do this honestly.

NEIGHBORHOOD EXPERTISE

I Know These Streets Because I Live on Them.

Short notes on the neighborhoods I work in most. Each is its own market, with its own rhythm and its own off-market culture.

Highland Park

MEDIAN $3.4M · 28 TRANSACTIONS YTD

The standard against which Dallas measures itself. Quiet streets, deeply established families, and an inventory that turns mostly off-market. Knowing who is preparing to sell, often a year before the listing, is the entire game here.

University Park

MEDIAN $2.1M · 41 TRANSACTIONS YTD

Family-anchored, walkable, and shaped around the university. Buyers care about block-by-block character, school feeders, and the calm rhythm of neighbors who have known each other for decades. I match clients to streets, not just houses.

Preston Hollow

MEDIAN $4.2M · 22 TRANSACTIONS YTD

Larger lots, longer setbacks, and a culture of privacy that prefers a quiet phone call to a marketing campaign. Here the work is mostly intuition and relationships. I represent both ends of the table with equal care.

Volk Estates

MEDIAN $3.8M · 11 TRANSACTIONS YTD

A small, deeply tenured pocket of University Park where homes rarely change hands and never do quickly. The work here is reading the few signals that suggest a listing is coming, and being the call that arrives first.

Bluffview

MEDIAN $2.6M · 19 TRANSACTIONS YTD

Rolling lots, mature trees, and an architecturally curious buyer pool. The streets reward an agent who knows which builders worked here in which decade and which renovations were done with restraint.

Prosper & Southlake

MEDIAN $2.8M · 34 TRANSACTIONS YTD

Newer estates, larger acreage, and clients arriving from the coasts who want space and stillness without leaving Dallas’s orbit. The buyer here is decisive, exacting, and rewards an agent who has actually walked the land.

HOW I WORK

Two Tables. The Same Standard of Care.

Every engagement begins the same way: an unhurried conversation. From there, the path differs depending on which side of the table you are on.

For Buyers

From First Conversation to Keys.

Most of my buyers are not in a hurry. We use that time well, and we never waste a showing.

  1. 01

    Private Consultation

    An unhurried conversation about timing, the life the house needs to hold, and the neighborhoods that fit. No pressure, no obligation, no buyer agreement until we both want one.

  2. 02

    Curated Search

    I send three to five homes, not thirty. The list includes off-market and pre-market inventory you will not see on the public sites. Each home is screened in person before I take you in.

  3. 03

    Offer and Negotiation

    Pricing strategy informed by recent comparable closings, not list prices. I write the offer, manage the back-and-forth, and pay attention to terms a strong listing agent will care about more than the headline number.

  4. 04

    Inspection to Close

    I attend the inspection with you. I have trusted relationships with structural engineers, foundation inspectors, and title attorneys when the situation calls for them. The closing table should be the calmest day of the process.

For Sellers

From Quiet Decision to Closed.

A great listing is engineered before the sign goes in the yard. Most of the work happens before the public ever sees the home.

  1. 01

    Confidential Valuation

    A private market analysis with three pricing scenarios, the supporting comparable sales, and an honest read of how your home will be received. No public footprint, no obligation.

  2. 02

    Pre-Market Preparation

    Staging, paint, light renovation when the math supports it. I coordinate with my preferred stager, contractor, and photographer. The home goes to market once, and only when it is ready.

  3. 03

    Quiet Launch and Listing

    Most of my listings are first shared with a curated network of agents and qualified buyers before they hit MLS. When we do go public, the photography, copy, and price are correct on day one.

  4. 04

    Offer Strategy and Closing

    I screen offers personally, advise on counters, and manage the contract through closing. You hear from me directly through every stage. There is no transaction coordinator standing between us.

CONCIERGE

Beyond the Transaction.

A house at this price point is rarely just bought or sold. The work around the work is what separates a $400K agent from a $4M agent.

i

Off-Market Access

A standing relationship with sellers who will entertain the right offer but will not list publicly. Access to the Briggs Freeman private network and the Sotheby’s International Realty global pipeline of pre-market inventory.

ii

Staging and Preparation

I cover the cost of professional staging on every listing I take above the seven-figure mark. My preferred stagers, painters, and contractors work to a calendar that respects your timeline, not theirs.

iii

Renovation Strategy

If a home should be lightly renovated before listing, I project-manage the work end to end with a builder I trust. If a buyer wants to renovate after closing, I introduce the architects and builders I would hire myself.

iv

Relocation Network

For clients moving to or from Dallas, I coordinate with vetted agents in New York, Aspen, Palm Beach, and the Bay Area through the Sotheby’s referral network. One conversation, one introduction, no cold outreach.

v

Discreet Photography and Marketing

An architectural photographer, a videographer, and a print designer who work to a brief, not a template. Your home is presented in the editorial language it deserves, with placements considered on a property-by-property basis.

vi

Trusted Advisors

Title attorneys, tax counsel, mortgage advisors at the private banks, foundation engineers, designers. The introductions you need at the moment you need them, with no quiet referral fee on either side.
