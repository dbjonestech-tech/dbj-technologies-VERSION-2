Practice Areas

What We Handle.

Auto Accidents

Rear-end collisions, intersection wrecks, and highway pile-ups. We pull police reports, scene photos, and dashcam evidence the day you sign, before the carrier reframes the story.

Truck and 18-Wheeler

Commercial trucking is governed by federal regulations the average defense firm rarely cites. We work with accident reconstructionists, ECM data, and FMCSA logs.

Motorcycle

Left-turn collisions, lane-change crashes, and underinsured motorist claims. Riders deserve a firm that does not start the case from the assumption that the rider was at fault.

Pedestrian and Bicycle

Crosswalk strikes, bike-lane collisions, and hit-and-run cases. Severe injuries are common and policy stacking matters. We pursue every available coverage layer.

Premises Liability

Serious falls, negligent security, and unsafe property conditions on commercial sites. We pursue the operator and the corporate owner, because liability often hides one entity removed from the scene.

Work Injury

Construction, oilfield, and warehouse injuries on Texas non-subscriber jobsites. Third-party negligence claims that workers' comp will not address.

Wrongful Death

Representing surviving spouses, parents, and children when negligence has taken a family member. We handle these cases with the discretion they deserve.

Dog Bites

Texas premises and one-bite-rule cases against homeowners and renters. Severe bite injuries, especially to children, are pursued under the property owner's insurance.

Catastrophic Injury

Traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord damage, severe burns, and amputations that change a life permanently. We coordinate life-care planning experts to quantify a lifetime of medical, vocational, and personal loss accurately.

Medical Malpractice and Bad Faith

Surgical errors, missed diagnoses, hospital negligence, and insurance carriers refusing to honor coverage they sold. These cases require expert review and a willingness to litigate against well-funded defense firms. We do not refer them out.

If You Were Just Hurt

Seven Things to Do After an Accident.

Most injury cases are won or lost in the first seventy-two hours. Save this list. If you can, do these in order before you talk to any insurance adjuster.

Get medical care, immediately.

Even if you feel okay. Adrenaline masks injury. A documented ER or urgent care visit anchors the medical record the carrier will scrutinize later.

Call the police and get a report number.

A formal report is hard to dispute. If officers did not respond, file a Texas Peace Officer's Crash Report (CR-2) the same day.

Photograph everything, on the scene.

Vehicle damage, the road, skid marks, traffic signals, weather, your injuries. Wide shots and close-ups. Time-stamped from your phone.

Get names and numbers for every witness.

Independent witnesses are gold in injury cases. Collect them yourself. By the time the police report arrives, half of them will have moved on.

Do not give a recorded statement.

The other driver's insurance company will call within forty-eight hours. They are not on your side. Politely decline until you have spoken with a lawyer.

Save every receipt and every record.

Medical bills, prescription costs, mileage to appointments, lost-wage documentation, repair estimates. Damages are proven on paper, not from memory.

Call a board-certified injury lawyer, free.

Before you sign a release, before you accept a check, before you give a statement. The consultation costs nothing. Mistakes made early cost everything.

Common Questions

What Injury Clients Ask First.

The questions below are the ones we field every week. If yours is not listed, call the firm or use the intake form. We answer plainly.

Do I have a case?
There is no flat answer until we hear the facts. The case-evaluation call is free. If we believe a recovery is realistic, we say so. If we do not, we tell you that as well, and we will often refer you to a firm better suited to your situation.
What does it cost to hire your firm?
Nothing up front. We work on a contingency fee, which means we are paid only if we recover money for you. The percentage is disclosed in plain language in the engagement agreement before you sign. If we do not win, you owe no attorney's fee.
How long will my case take?
Simple auto cases can resolve in three to nine months. Complex trucking, malpractice, or wrongful death cases often run twelve to twenty-four months, sometimes longer if trial is required. We will not settle a case prematurely just because the carrier offers a fast number.
What if I was partially at fault?
Texas follows a modified comparative negligence rule. As long as you were fifty percent or less at fault, you can still recover. Your recovery is reduced by your percentage of fault. Do not assume you have no case because the other side is blaming you. Adjusters do that on every claim.
What if the other driver had no insurance?
Your own auto policy may include uninsured or underinsured motorist coverage. Most Texas drivers have it and do not realize it. We pull every available policy, including stacked household coverage, and pursue all of them when fault and damages support it.
Can I switch lawyers if I already signed with another firm?
Yes. You have the right to fire and replace your attorney at any time. The original firm may have a lien for work done, which is paid out of the recovery and does not increase your total fee. We have taken cases mid-stream and improved them. Call us before you do anything else.
Is the consultation really free?
Yes. There is no charge to call, no charge to meet, no charge to review records, and no obligation to hire us. If you decide to retain the firm, the engagement agreement is on a no-recovery-no-fee basis.
