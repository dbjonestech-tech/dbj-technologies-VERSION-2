Our Approach

We Cook What the Season Gives Us.

Our menu changes with what is good right now. In October, that means roots and stone fruit and the last of the heirloom tomatoes. In February, citrus and brassicas and slow braises. We do not chase trends. We cook what tastes best on the day you sit down.

Chef Marcus Cole spent a decade in New York and the Bay Area before coming home to Dallas. He learned to cook in kitchens that respected the ingredient, that did not bury good food under technique, that knew when to step back.

The best compliment we get is that the food tastes like itself. The carrot tastes like a carrot. The fish tastes like the gulf. We work hard to make that happen, and we work hard to make it look effortless.

From the Kitchen

Chef Marcus Cole

12 Years · New York · San Francisco · Dallas

Marcus spent twelve years in fine-dining kitchens across New York and San Francisco before coming home to Dallas. Ember & Vine is the restaurant he always wanted to open: serious food without the stiffness, plated by a chef who still works the line every service. Most nights, that is him at the pass with the tweezers.

“Cooking for strangers is the most generous thing I can think of.”

The Wine Program

Chosen, Not Collected.

Our list is sixty bottles deep, not three hundred. We pour what we love drinking and what plays well with our food. You will find small producers from Piedmont and Burgundy alongside cult Texas growers and a few quiet bottles that pay off if you trust us.

Half the list is by the glass, because not every dinner is a four-course commitment. Our somm spends his weekends visiting growers. If you ask him what to drink, he will ask you three questions and pour something honest.
